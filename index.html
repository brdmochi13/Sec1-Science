Tier 1⚖️ Density Calculations & Floating/Sinking▼
FormulaDensity (ρ) = Mass (m) ÷ Volume (V)  |  Unit: g/cm³ or kg/m³

Floating / Sinking Rule

Object density < liquid density → FLOATS Object density > liquid density → SINKS Object density = liquid density → SUSPENDED
⚠️ Steel ship paradox (Paper 3, Q2)Steel (7.87 g/cm³) is denser than water (1.0 g/cm³) yet ships float — because the overall density of the ship (steel + air inside) is less than water. The ship's shape traps air, reducing average density.

Worked Example (Paper 8 style)

Step-by-step calculationWood block: mass = 200g, dimensions = 10cm × 5cm × 4cm
Step 1: V = l × w × h = 10 × 5 × 4 = 200 cm³
Step 2: ρ = m/V = 200/200 = 1.0 g/cm³
Step 3: Compare with water (1.0 g/cm³) → density equal → object is at the boundary (just floats)
Always show ALL 3 steps for full marks!

Volume by Displacement (irregular solids)

✅ Method1. Measure initial water volume (V₁) in measuring cylinder
2. Tie string to object, gently lower into cylinder
3. Measure final water volume (V₂)
4. Volume of object = V₂ − V₁
Must measure mass BEFORE placing in water (mass reading becomes inaccurate if object is wet)

Tier 1🔬 Separation Techniques▼

All 5 Techniques — When & How

TechniqueSeparatesPrincipleReal-life use
Magnetic attractionMagnetic solid from non-magneticOnly iron/steel/nickel attracted by magnetRecycling scrap metal, removing iron from food
FiltrationInsoluble solid from liquidSolid particles too large to pass through filter paper poresRemoving sand from water, sewage treatment
EvaporationDissolved solid from solutionLiquid evaporates (boils off), solid remainsSalt from seawater, ikan bilis preservation

⚠️ Exam Language TrapsResidue = solid LEFT on filter paper (NOT the liquid)  |  Filtrate = liquid that PASSES through filter paper  |  Distillate = liquid collected AFTER condensation in distillation  |  NOT the substance remaining in flask!

Multi-step Separation (most common Section C type)

Iron + sand + salt mixture — standard sequence:
Step 1: Magnetic attraction → remove iron filings
Step 2: Add water, stir → salt dissolves; sand does NOT
Step 3: Filtration → remove sand (residue); salt solution passes through (filtrate)
Step 4: Evaporation → evaporate water, collect solid salt
(If pure water needed → distillation instead of evaporation at Step 4)

Chromatography — Key Terms

Solvent front = how far solvent travels The HIGHEST spot = most soluble in solvent Number of spots = number of pigments present Chromatogram = the paper after experiment
✅ Singapore Context: NEWater (Paper 7)3 steps: (1) Microfiltration (removes solids) → (2) Reverse osmosis (removes dissolved salts, bacteria) → (3) UV disinfection (kills remaining microorganisms)
Singapore's 4 National Taps: Local catchment, Imported water, NEWater, Desalinated water
Tier 1🔭 Scientific Method & Investigation▼

Variables — Definitions

VariableDefinitionExample (dissolving experiment)
Independent Variable (IV)The variable you CHANGE (only 1)Temperature of water
Dependent Variable (DV)The variable you MEASURE/OBSERVETime taken for salt to dissolve
Controlled Variables (CV)Everything else kept CONSTANT for fair testAmount of salt, amount of water, stirring rate
✅ Hypothesis Format (must use IF-THEN)"If [IV] increases, then [DV] will [increase/decrease] because [reason]."
Example: "If the temperature of water increases, then the rate of dissolving of salt will increase because higher temperature gives particles more energy to move faster and collide more."

Steps of Scientific Method (in order)

1. Observe/identify problem 2. Formulate hypothesis 3. Plan & carry out experiment 4. Collect data 5. Analyse/interpret data 6. Draw conclusions
📌 If results do NOT support hypothesis:Do NOT say "my hypothesis is wrong and I give up." Correct answer: "I will re-examine the hypothesis and modify it based on the evidence gathered, then design a new experiment to test it."

Accuracy vs Precision

TermMeaning

Tier 2🔬 Atoms, Molecules & Periodic Table▼

Atom Structure (G2 level)

Atom = electrically neutral; positively charged nucleus (protons + neutrons) + negatively charged electrons orbiting around it. Each element has a unique number of protons (atomic number).

Molecule

A group of two or more atoms chemically combined. E.g. O₂ = 2 oxygen atoms; H₂O = 2 hydrogen + 1 oxygen atom.

Periodic Table

Period = horizontal row Group = vertical column Same group = similar chemical properties
⛔ Writing chemical formulae NOT required in G2You need to COUNT atoms from given formulae but do NOT need to write them. H₂O → 2 H atoms + 1 O atom.
Tier 2💡 Ray Model of Light▼

Reflection

Law: Angle of incidence = Angle of reflection (both measured from the NORMAL, not the surface)
Specular reflection: smooth surface → parallel reflected rays (mirrors)
Diffuse reflection: rough surface → scattered reflected rays (paper, walls)

Mirrors

MirrorImage typeUse
Plane mirrorVirtual, upright, same size, laterally invertedBathroom mirror
Convex mirrorVirtual, upright, smaller — wider field of viewCar side mirrors, security mirrors
Concave mirrorReal or virtual depending on object positionTorch reflector, dentist's mirror
Refraction (optional for G2 but may appear)Light bends when entering a different medium. Into denser medium (air→glass) = bends TOWARD normal. Into less dense (glass→air) = bends AWAY from normal. No angle calculations required for G2.
EM Radiation — beneficial and harmfulInfrared: remote controls (beneficial); excessive heat (harmful)
Ultraviolet: vitamin D production (beneficial); skin cancer (harmful)
Light: vision (beneficial); light pollution (harmful)

Section B True/False — Answer Template
Always use this structure for full marks"[True/False] because [scientific principle that directly explains why]."

Example: "Distillation separates substances based on differences in their particle sizes."
→ ✗ False because particle sizes are used in filtration.
→ ✓ False because distillation separates substances based on their different BOILING POINTS, not particle sizes.
Half-mark trapIf you write the correct T/F but wrong reason, or correct reason without stating T/F — you lose HALF the marks. Always write both.
